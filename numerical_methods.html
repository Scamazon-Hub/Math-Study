Numerical Methods

Why Use Numerical Methods?

In the real world, many engineering problems don't have neat, exact solutions. Numerical methods are techniques used to find approximate solutions to these complex problems using basic arithmetic operations. They are the basis of computer simulations and modern engineering software.

1. Numerical Integration

When to Use

To find the approximate area under a curve when the function is too difficult to integrate analytically, or when you only have a set of measured data points (e.g., from an experiment).

The Trapezoidal Rule

This method approximates the area by dividing it into a number of trapezoids.

$$ \int_{a}^{b} y \, dx \approx \frac{h}{2} [y_0 + y_n + 2(y_1 + y_2 + ... + y_{n-1})] $$

Simpson's Rule

This method approximates the area using parabolas, which is often more accurate. It requires an **even number of strips**.

$$ \int_{a}^{b} y \, dx \approx \frac{h}{3} [y_0 + y_n + 4(\text{sum of odd } y\text{'s}) + 2(\text{sum of even } y\text{'s})] $$

Example: Approximate $$ \int_{0}^{4} x^2 dx $$ using 4 strips.

Step 1: Find strip width (h) and y-values.
$$ h = (4-0)/4 = 1 $$. The ordinates are:
$$y_0 = 0^2 = 0$$ $$y_1 = 1^2 = 1$$ $$y_2 = 2^2 = 4$$ $$y_3 = 3^2 = 9$$ $$y_4 = 4^2 = 16$$

Step 2: Apply the Trapezoidal Rule.
$$ A \approx \frac{1}{2}[y_0 + y_4 + 2(y_1+y_2+y_3)] = \frac{1}{2}[0 + 16 + 2(1+4+9)] = \frac{1}{2}[16+28] = 22 $$

Step 3: Apply Simpson's Rule.
$$ A \approx \frac{1}{3}[y_0 + y_4 + 4(y_1+y_3) + 2(y_2)] = \frac{1}{3}[0 + 16 + 4(1+9) + 2(4)] = \frac{1}{3}[16+40+8] = \frac{64}{3} \approx 21.333 $$

Conclusion: The exact analytical answer is 21.333. For this function, Simpson's rule gives the exact answer, demonstrating its higher accuracy.

2. Finding Roots of Equations

Newton-Raphson Method

When to Use

To find successively better approximations to the roots (or zeroes) of a real-valued function. It's an iterative process that uses the tangent line to find the next guess. It is very powerful but requires the function's derivative.

Iterative Formula: $$ x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)} $$

Example: Find a root of $$f(x) = x^2 - 2$$, starting with $$x_0=1$$.

1. Find the derivative: $$ f'(x) = 2x $$

2. First iteration (to find x₁):
$$ x_1 = x_0 - \frac{f(x_0)}{f'(x_0)} = 1 - \frac{1^2-2}{2(1)} = 1 - \frac{-1}{2} = 1.5 $$

3. Second iteration (to find x₂):
$$ x_2 = x_1 - \frac{f(x_1)}{f'(x_1)} = 1.5 - \frac{(1.5)^2-2}{2(1.5)} = 1.5 - \frac{0.25}{3} \approx 1.4167 $$

Final Answer: After two iterations, the root is approximately 1.4167. (The actual root is $$\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4142$$).

3. Numerical Solutions for Differential Equations

Euler's Method

When to Use

To find an approximate solution to a first-order ordinary differential equation when an analytical solution is not possible. It is the simplest method for this type of problem.

Given $$ \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x,y) $$ with an initial value $$y(x_0)=y_0$$, we can find the next value using a small step size, h.

Iterative Formula: $$ y_{n+1} = y_n + h \cdot f(x_n, y_n) $$

Example: Given $$ \frac{dy}{dx} = x - y $$ with initial value $$y(0)=1$$. Use Euler's method with a step size of $$h=0.1$$ to estimate $$y(0.2)$$.

Step 1 (Find y₁ at x₁=0.1):
Here, $$x_0=0, y_0=1$$. The slope at this point is $$f(x_0, y_0) = 0 - 1 = -1$$.
$$ y_1 = y_0 + h \cdot f(x_0, y_0) = 1 + (0.1)(-1) = 0.9 $$

Step 2 (Find y₂ at x₂=0.2):
Now, $$x_1=0.1, y_1=0.9$$. The slope at this new point is $$f(x_1, y_1) = 0.1 - 0.9 = -0.8$$.
$$ y_2 = y_1 + h \cdot f(x_1, y_1) = 0.9 + (0.1)(-0.8) = 0.9 - 0.08 = 0.82 $$

Final Answer: The approximate value is $$y(0.2) \approx 0.82$$.
