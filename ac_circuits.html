AC Circuit Analysis

Using Complex Numbers in AC Circuits

Complex numbers are essential for analyzing AC circuits because they can represent both the amplitude and phase of a sinusoidal waveform in a single number (a phasor). This simplifies calculations by turning trigonometric problems into algebra.

1. AC Waveform Characteristics

General Form

The instantaneous value of a current or voltage can be expressed as $$ v(t) = V_m\sin(\omega t + \phi) $$.

Key Formulas: Angular Frequency $$\omega = 2\pi f$$. Period $$T = 1/f = 2\pi/\omega$$.

RMS Value: The Root Mean Square (RMS) value is the effective DC equivalent for power delivery. For a sine wave, $$ V_{RMS} = \frac{V_m}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707 V_m $$. Unless stated otherwise, AC voltage and current values are typically given as RMS.

Example: An AC current is given by $$i = 43\sin(50\pi t + 0.6)$$. Find its key properties.

a) Amplitude (Peak Current): $$ I_m = 43 $$ A.

b) RMS Current: $$ I_{RMS} = \frac{43}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 30.41 $$ A.

c) Angular Frequency ($$\omega$$): From the equation, $$ \omega = 50\pi $$ rad/s.

d) Frequency (f): $$ f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ Hz} $$.

e) Period (T): $$ T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{25} = 0.04 \text{ s} = 40 \text{ ms}$$.

f) Phase Angle ($$\phi$$): $$ \phi = 0.6 \text{ rad} $$. In degrees: $$ 0.6 \times \frac{180}{\pi} \approx 34.38^\circ $$.

2. Impedance (Z) and Admittance (Y)

What is Impedance?

Impedance ($$Z$$) is the total opposition to current flow in an AC circuit. It is a complex quantity with a real part (Resistance, R) and an imaginary part (Reactance, X). Admittance ($$Y$$) is the reciprocal of impedance ($$Y=1/Z$$), useful for parallel circuits.

Impedance of Components

The impedance of the three basic passive components depends on the angular frequency $$\omega$$.

ComponentImpedance ($$Z$$)Description
Resistor (R)$$Z_R = R$$Real, no phase shift.
Inductor (L)$$Z_L = j\omega L$$Imaginary, causes voltage to lead current by 90°.
Capacitor (C)$$Z_C = \frac{1}{j\omega C} = -j\frac{1}{\omega C}$$Imaginary, causes current to lead voltage by 90°.

Example: A circuit has R=20$$\Omega$$, L=10mH, and C=500$$\mu$$F in series at a frequency of 100 Hz. Find the total impedance.

Step 1: Find angular frequency $$\omega$$.
$$ \omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(100) \approx 628.3 \text{ rad/s} $$

Step 2: Calculate individual impedances.
$$ Z_R = 20 \, \Omega $$
$$ Z_L = j\omega L = j(628.3)(0.01) \approx 6.28j \, \Omega $$
$$ Z_C = -j\frac{1}{\omega C} = -j\frac{1}{628.3 \times 500 \times 10^{-6}} \approx -3.18j \, \Omega $$

Step 3: Sum the impedances for the series circuit.
$$ Z_{total} = Z_R + Z_L + Z_C = 20 + 6.28j - 3.18j = 20 + 3.1j \, \Omega $$

Example: Given parallel impedances $$ Z_1 = 9+2j $$ and $$ Z_2 = -12+8j $$, find the total equivalent impedance $$Z_T = \frac{Z_1Z_2}{Z_1+Z_2}$$.

Step 1: Sum $$Z_1+Z_2$$.
$$ (9+2j) + (-12+8j) = -3 + 10j $$

Step 2: Product $$Z_1Z_2$$.
$$ (9+2j)(-12+8j) = -108 + 72j - 24j + 16j^2 = -124 + 48j $$

Step 3: Perform the division.
$$ Z_T = \frac{-124+48j}{-3+10j} = \frac{-124+48j}{-3+10j} \times \frac{-3-10j}{-3-10j} = \frac{372+1240j-144j-480j^2}{9+100} = \frac{852+1096j}{109} $$

Rectangular form: $$ Z_T \approx 7.82 + 10.05j \, \Omega$$

3. Power in AC Circuits & The Power Triangle

Understanding AC Power

In AC circuits, not all power delivered by the source is used to do work. We use the power triangle to understand the relationship between Real, Reactive, and Apparent power.
  • Real Power (P): The actual power dissipated by resistive components, measured in **Watts (W)**. $$P = S \cos(\phi)$$.
  • Reactive Power (Q): Power exchanged with reactive components (inductors/capacitors), measured in **Volt-Amps Reactive (VAR)**. $$Q = S \sin(\phi)$$.
  • Apparent Power (S): The total power that appears to be delivered, measured in **Volt-Amps (VA)**. It's the vector sum of P and Q. $$S = |V_{RMS}||I_{RMS}|$$.
  • Power Factor (pf): The ratio of real power to apparent power. $$ pf = \cos(\phi) = P/S $$.

Example: Given an RMS voltage of $$ V = 36+29j $$ volts and an RMS current of $$ I=7+4j $$ amps, analyze the circuit's power.

Step 1: Convert V and I to polar form to find magnitudes and angles.
$$ |V| = \sqrt{36^2+29^2} \approx 46.23 \text{ V} $$
$$ \theta_V = \tan^{-1}(29/36) \approx 38.81^\circ $$
So, $$ V \approx 46.23\angle38.81^\circ \text{ V} $$

$$ |I| = \sqrt{7^2+4^2} \approx 8.06 \text{ A} $$
$$ \theta_I = \tan^{-1}(4/7) \approx 29.74^\circ $$
So, $$ I \approx 8.06\angle29.74^\circ \text{ A} $$

Step 2: Calculate the phase difference ($$\phi$$). This is the angle of the impedance.
$$ \phi = \theta_V - \theta_I = 38.81^\circ - 29.74^\circ = 9.07^\circ $$

Step 3: Calculate the different types of power.
Apparent Power (S): $$ S = |V||I| = (46.23)(8.06) \approx 372.6 \text{ VA} $$
Real Power (P): $$ P = S\cos(\phi) = 372.6 \times \cos(9.07^\circ) \approx 368.0 \text{ W} $$
Reactive Power (Q): $$ Q = S\sin(\phi) = 372.6 \times \sin(9.07^\circ) \approx 58.8 \text{ VAR} $$

Step 4: Determine the Power Factor (pf).
$$ pf = \cos(9.07^\circ) \approx 0.987 $$
Since $$\phi$$ is positive (voltage angle > current angle), the circuit is inductive and the power factor is **0.987 lagging**.
