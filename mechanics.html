Applied Mechanics & Kinematics

1. Newton's Laws of Motion

The Foundation of Mechanics

These three laws describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, and its motion in response to those forces.
  • First Law (Inertia): An object remains at rest or continues in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by a net external force.
  • Second Law (Force & Acceleration): The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass. $$ F = ma $$
  • Third Law (Action-Reaction): For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Example (Second Law):

A net force of 200 N is applied to a 50 kg crate. Find the acceleration of the crate.

$$ F = ma \implies a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{200 \text{ N}}{50 \text{ kg}} = 4 \text{ m/s}^2 $$

2. Equations of Motion (SUVAT)

When to Use

Use these equations for motion in a straight line with **constant acceleration**. The variables are:
s = displacement, u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time.

$$ v = u + at \quad | \quad s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \quad | \quad v^2 = u^2 + 2as $$

Example:

A ball is projected vertically upwards at 36km/h from a 108m tall building. Find the time taken to reach the ground (g = 9.8 m/s²).

Step 1: Convert units. $$ u = 36 \frac{\text{km}}{\text{h}} = 10 \text{ m/s} $$

Step 2: List variables (taking upwards as positive).
$$ s = -108 \text{ m} $$ (The final displacement is 108m below the start point)
$$ u = +10 \text{ m/s} $$
$$ a = -9.8 \text{ m/s}^2 $$ (Gravity acts downwards)
$$ t = ? $$

Step 3: Choose equation and substitute. $$ s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 $$
$$ -108 = 10t + \frac{1}{2}(-9.8)t^2 \implies -108 = 10t - 4.9t^2 $$

Step 4: Rearrange to a standard quadratic equation. $$ 4.9t^2 - 10t - 108 = 0 $$

Step 5: Solve the quadratic for t. Using the formula $$ t = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a} $$
$$ t = \frac{10 \pm \sqrt{(-10)^2 - 4(4.9)(-108)}}{2(4.9)} = \frac{10 \pm \sqrt{100 + 2116.8}}{9.8} = \frac{10 \pm 47.08}{9.8} $$

Step 6: Choose the valid solution. Since time must be positive, $$ t = \frac{10 + 47.08}{9.8} \approx 5.82 \text{ s} $$

3. Circular Motion

Key Concepts

An object moving in a circle at a constant speed is accelerating because its direction is constantly changing. This **centripetal acceleration** is always directed towards the center of the circle and is caused by a **centripetal force**.

Angular vs. Linear Speed: $$ v = r\omega $$

Centripetal Acceleration ($$a_c$$): $$ a_c = \omega^2r = \frac{v^2}{r} $$

Centripetal Force ($$F_c$$): $$ F_c = m\omega^2r = \frac{mv^2}{r} $$

Example: Steam Engine Governor

A governor has two spheres on rods 60cm long. Find the rotational speed in rpm when the rods make an angle of 20° with the vertical shaft.

1. Equate forces: The horizontal component of tension provides the centripetal force ($$T\sin\theta = m\omega^2r$$), while the vertical component supports the weight ($$T\cos\theta = mg$$). Dividing these gives $$ \tan\theta = \frac{\omega^2r}{g} $$.

2. Use geometry: The radius of rotation is $$ r = L\sin\theta = 0.6\sin(20^\circ) $$.

3. Solve for $$\omega$$: $$ \tan(20^\circ) = \frac{\omega^2(0.6\sin(20^\circ))}{9.81} \implies \omega^2 = \frac{9.81 \tan(20^\circ)}{0.6\sin(20^\circ)} \approx 17.4 $$
$$ \omega = \sqrt{17.4} \approx 4.17 \text{ rad/s} $$.

4. Convert to rpm: $$ \text{rpm} = 4.17 \frac{\text{rad}}{\text{s}} \times \frac{60}{2\pi} \approx 39.8 \text{ rpm} $$

4. Work, Energy, and Power

Key Concepts

These concepts link forces with changes in motion and position.
  • Work Done (W): The energy transferred when a force causes displacement. $$W=Fd$$. Measured in Joules (J).
  • Kinetic Energy (KE): The energy of an object due to its motion. $$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$.
  • Potential Energy (PE): Stored energy due to an object's position in a gravitational field. $$PE = mgh$$.
  • Power (P): The rate at which work is done. $$P = \frac{W}{t} = Fv$$. Measured in Watts (W).

Example: A 1200 kg car accelerates from 10 m/s to 30 m/s. Find the work done by the engine and the average power developed if this takes 8 seconds.

1. Find the change in kinetic energy (Work-Energy Principle):
The work done is equal to the change in KE.
$$ W = \Delta KE = KE_{final} - KE_{initial} = \frac{1}{2}m(v^2 - u^2) $$
$$ W = \frac{1}{2}(1200)(30^2 - 10^2) = 600(900-100) = 600(800) = 480,000 \text{ J} $$

2. Calculate the average power:
$$ P = \frac{\text{Work Done}}{\text{time}} = \frac{480000 \text{ J}}{8 \text{ s}} = 60,000 \text{ W} = 60 \text{ kW} $$
