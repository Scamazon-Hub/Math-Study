Statistics for Engineering

1. Descriptive Statistics

When to Use

Use these measures to summarize and understand the key features of a dataset, such as its central tendency and dispersion. This is the first step in any data analysis.

Mean ($$\bar{x}$$): The average value. $$ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} $$

Median: The middle value when the data is sorted.

Mode: The most frequently occurring value.

Standard Deviation ($$\sigma$$): Measures how spread out the data is from the mean. $$ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x-\bar{x})^2}{n}} $$

Example: Find the mean and standard deviation for the dataset of pressure readings: {10, 12, 13, 12, 15}.

1. Calculate the mean: $$ \bar{x} = \frac{10+12+13+12+15}{5} = \frac{62}{5} = 12.4 $$

2. Calculate the sum of squared differences:
$$ (10-12.4)^2 + (12-12.4)^2 + (13-12.4)^2 + (12-12.4)^2 + (15-12.4)^2 $$ $$ = (-2.4)^2 + (-0.4)^2 + (0.6)^2 + (-0.4)^2 + (2.6)^2 $$ $$ = 5.76 + 0.16 + 0.36 + 0.16 + 6.76 = 13.2 $$

3. Calculate the standard deviation: $$ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{13.2}{5}} = \sqrt{2.64} \approx 1.62 $$

2. The Normal Distribution

When to Use

For continuous data that clusters symmetrically around a mean, like manufacturing tolerances or measurement errors. It is the most important probability distribution in statistics.

The **Standardised Z-score** converts any normal distribution into a standard one (with mean=0, std dev=1), allowing us to find probabilities using Z-tables.

$$ Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} $$

Example: A component has a mean length ($$\mu$$) of 100mm with a standard deviation ($$\sigma$$) of 0.2mm. What percentage of components are longer than 100.5mm?

1. Find the Z-score for x = 100.5mm:
$$ Z = \frac{100.5 - 100}{0.2} = \frac{0.5}{0.2} = 2.5 $$

2. Find the cumulative probability for Z=2.5.
Using a Z-table or calculator, a Z-score of 2.5 corresponds to a cumulative probability of 0.9938. This means 99.38% of components are shorter than 100.5mm.

3. Calculate the required probability.
The probability of a component being *longer* is $$ 1 - P(Z < 2.5) $$.
$$ 1 - 0.9938 = 0.0062 $$

Conclusion: 0.62% of the components are expected to be longer than 100.5mm.

3. Linear Correlation & Regression

When to Use

Use correlation to measure the strength of a linear relationship between two variables. Use regression to create a formula (the line of best fit) to make predictions.

Line of Best Fit: $$ y = a + bx $$

Gradient (b): $$ b = \frac{n(\sum xy) - (\sum x)(\sum y)}{n(\sum x^2) - (\sum x)^2} $$

Y-intercept (a): $$ a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x} $$

Example: Given the following data for Temperature (x) and Strength (y), find the line of best fit.

Data: (10, 45), (20, 42), (30, 38), (40, 34), (50, 31)

1. Calculate summary values:
$$n=5, \quad \sum x = 150, \quad \sum y = 190, \quad \sum x^2 = 5500, \quad \sum xy = 5310$$

2. Calculate the gradient (b):
$$ b = \frac{5(5310) - (150)(190)}{5(5500) - (150)^2} = \frac{26550 - 28500}{27500 - 22500} = \frac{-1950}{5000} = -0.39 $$

3. Calculate the y-intercept (a):
First find means: $$ \bar{x} = 150/5 = 30, \quad \bar{y} = 190/5 = 38 $$.
$$ a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x} = 38 - (-0.39)(30) = 38 + 11.7 = 49.7 $$

Line of Best Fit: $$ y = 49.7 - 0.39x $$

4. Basic Probability

When to Use

To quantify the likelihood of an event occurring, essential for risk assessment and quality control.

Addition Rule (A or B): $$ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) $$

Multiplication Rule (A and B, independent): $$ P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B) $$

Conditional Probability (A given B): $$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $$

Example (Addition Rule): The probability of a component failing due to heat is 0.05. The probability of it failing due to vibration is 0.03. The probability of it failing due to both is 0.01. What is the probability it fails due to heat or vibration?

$$ P(H \cup V) = P(H) + P(V) - P(H \cap V) = 0.05 + 0.03 - 0.01 = 0.07 $$

Answer: There is a 7% chance of failure from either cause.
