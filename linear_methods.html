Further Algebra & Linear Methods

1. Solving Systems of Equations using Matrices

When to Use

Use matrices to solve systems of simultaneous linear equations, which are common in circuit analysis (e.g., Kirchhoff's laws) and structural analysis (e.g., forces in a truss).

2x2 Systems (Inverse Matrix Method)

For a system $$AX=B$$, the solution is $$X = A^{-1}B$$.

Example: Solve the system $$ 2x+3y=8, x+4y=9 $$

1. Matrix Form: $$ \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 1 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 9 \end{pmatrix} $$

2. Determinant: $$ \det(A) = (2)(4) - (3)(1) = 5 $$

3. Inverse: $$ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{pmatrix} 4 & -3 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} $$

4. Solve $$X = A^{-1}B$$: $$ \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{pmatrix} 4 & -3 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 9 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{5}\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 10 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} $$

Final Answer: $$ x=1, y=2 $$.

3x3 Systems (Cramer's Rule)

Cramer's rule is an efficient method for solving 3x3 systems. The solution for each variable is a ratio of determinants.

$$ x = \frac{\det(A_x)}{\det(A)}, \quad y = \frac{\det(A_y)}{\det(A)}, \quad z = \frac{\det(A_z)}{\det(A)} $$

Example: Solve $$ x+y+z=6, 2x-y+z=3, x+2y-z=2 $$

1. Find the main determinant, $$\det(A)$$: $$ \det(A) = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = 1(1-2) - 1(-2-1) + 1(4 - (-1)) = -1+3+5=7 $$

2. Find $$\det(A_x)$$: (Replace x-column with B vector)
$$ \det(A_x) = \begin{vmatrix} 6 & 1 & 1 \\ 3 & -1 & 1 \\ 2 & 2 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = 6(1-2) - 1(-3-2) + 1(6 - (-2)) = -6+5+8=7 $$

3. Solve for x: $$ x = \frac{\det(A_x)}{\det(A)} = \frac{7}{7} = 1 $$

(Similarly, one would find $$ \det(A_y)=14 $$ and $$ \det(A_z)=21 $$)

Final Answer: $$ x=1, y=2, z=3 $$.

2. Vector Operations

When to Use

To represent quantities with both magnitude and direction, like force, velocity, and displacement.

Dot (Scalar) Product: Results in a scalar. Use to find the work done or the angle between two vectors.

$$ \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = |\mathbf{A}| |\mathbf{B}| \cos\theta = A_xB_x + A_yB_y + A_zB_z $$

Cross (Vector) Product: Results in a new vector perpendicular to the original two. Use to find torque or moments.

$$ \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ A_x & A_y & A_z \\ B_x & B_y & B_z \end{vmatrix} $$

Example: For vectors $$ \mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} $$ and $$ \mathbf{B} = 4\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} $$, find the angle between them.

1. Find the dot product: $$ \mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} = (2)(4) + (3)(-1) = 8 - 3 = 5 $$

2. Find the magnitude of each vector:
$$ |\mathbf{A}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{13} $$
$$ |\mathbf{B}| = \sqrt{4^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{17} $$

3. Find the angle: $$ \theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}}{|\mathbf{A}||\mathbf{B}|}\right) = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{5}{\sqrt{13}\sqrt{17}}\right) \approx 70.35^\circ $$

Example: Find the cross product of $$ \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k} $$ and $$ \mathbf{B} = 4\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k} $$

$$ \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{vmatrix} = \mathbf{i}(12-15) - \mathbf{j}(6-12) + \mathbf{k}(5-8) $$

Final Answer: $$ -3\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k} $$

3. Partial Fractions

When to Use

To decompose a complex algebraic fraction into simpler parts. This is an essential step before integrating many rational functions or finding an inverse Laplace transform.

Case 1: Distinct Linear Factors

$$ \frac{px+q}{(x-a)(x-b)} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{x-b} $$

Example: Decompose $$ \frac{5x-1}{(x-2)(x+1)} $$

$$ \frac{5x-1}{(x-2)(x+1)} = \frac{A}{x-2} + \frac{B}{x+1} \implies 5x-1 = A(x+1) + B(x-2) $$

Let $$x=2 \implies 9 = 3A \implies A=3$$.

Let $$x=-1 \implies -6 = -3B \implies B=2$$.

Answer: $$ \frac{3}{x-2} + \frac{2}{x+1} $$

Case 2: Repeated Linear Factors

$$ \frac{px+q}{(x-a)^2} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{B}{(x-a)^2} $$

Example: Decompose $$ \frac{2x+3}{(x-1)^2} $$

$$ \frac{2x+3}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{B}{(x-1)^2} \implies 2x+3 = A(x-1) + B $$

Let $$x=1 \implies 5 = B$$.

Equating coefficients of x: $$ 2 = A $$.

Answer: $$ \frac{2}{x-1} + \frac{5}{(x-1)^2} $$

Case 3: Irreducible Quadratic Factors

$$ \frac{px+q}{(x-a)(x^2+c)} = \frac{A}{x-a} + \frac{Bx+C}{x^2+c} $$

Example: Decompose $$ \frac{3x+2}{(x+1)(x^2+4)} $$

$$ 3x+2 = A(x^2+4) + (Bx+C)(x+1) $$

Let $$x=-1 \implies -1 = 5A \implies A=-1/5 $$.

Equating coefficients of $$x^2$$: $$ 0 = A+B \implies B = -A = 1/5 $$.

Equating constant terms: $$ 2 = 4A+C \implies C = 2-4A = 2 - 4(-1/5) = 14/5 $$.

Answer: $$ \frac{-1/5}{x+1} + \frac{(1/5)x + 14/5}{x^2+4} $$
